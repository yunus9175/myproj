Our Treatments

TURP

Transurethral Resection Of The Prostate

  • This is an endoscopic procedure for treatment of prostatic enlargement (BEP).
  • There are no external cuts or stitches
  • The enlarged / obstructing portion of the gland is removed by cutting it into small bits endoscopically with cautery or Laser
  • Procedure lasts for one to one and half hours
  • There is a catheter kept for 2 days
  • Hospital stay is for 2 to 3 days
  • There can be burning, urgency and frequency for a couple of days
  • Patient can be back to work in 5 to 7 days time

URS

Ureterorenoscopy



  • This is a endoscopic procedure to remove stones stuck in the lower or middle part of the ureter
  • There are no cuts or stitches
  • Endoscope is passed into the ureter upto the stone
  • Stone broken into small fragments with lithoclast or Laser.
  • Fragments removed
  • Sometimes a stent needs to be kept if stone is very big or if the ureter is very narrow
  • Hospital stay is 24 to 36 hrs
  • Patient can back to work in 3 days

PCNL

Percutaneous Nephro Lithotomy


  • This is an endoscopic procedure to remove stones from the kidney.
  • Here a small one cm cut is taken on the back, at the level of the kidney
  • If multiple stones are present in different parts of the kidney, two or more cuts may be required
  • Through the cut a small endoscope is passed to reach the stone
  • The stones are broken into smaller pieces and removed
  • Occasionally a stent may need to be kept
  • A small tube is left at the site of the cut for a day or two
  • Procedure time is 2 to 3 hrs depending on stone size
  • Hospital stay is 3 to 4 days
  • Bleeding and infection can occur rarely
  • The small cut heals within 3 to 4 days, no stitch is required

ESWL

LITHOTRIPSY



  • This is a procedure by which small stones in the kidney and the upper part of the ureter are removed by crushing without operation
  • Stones upto 15 to 18 mm can be treated this way
  • No anaesthesia or operation required
  • No admission required
  • Procedure takes 45 min, after which person can walk back to work
  • Electro magnetic waves produced by the machine used to fragment the stones
  • Sometimes a stent needs to be put before lithotripsy, if the stone is big or tightly stuck
  • No need for rest
  • One or two sittings may be required
  • Fragmented stones pass off in the urine as a fine powder within 8 to 10 days
